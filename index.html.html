Edible Flowers That You Can Grow In Your Garden

There are far more edible flower species than you think. Here's a word to the wise - NEVER eat anything unless you can be absolutely sure that you have identified it correctly, and always ensure that you carefully explain to your kids about which flowers are edible and which are not. Using edible flowers in recipes for food and drinks offers a wide range of benefits, which indludes intensifying flavors, providing texture and crunch, offering up a pleasant aroma, adding a punch of nutrition, allowing you to convey emotions and energies, and evoking valuable memories. Edible flowers can be used in teas, candies, chocolates and various dishes. It can also be used to as decoration. You can decorate cakes or cupcakes by putting candied rose petals onto the icing, for example. The most commonly used edible flowers used for food and decoration are Roses, Violets, Lavender, Bachelor's Button, Nasturtiums, Pansies, Carnations, English Daisies and Hibiscus.

Flowers With A Pleasant Scent

Not all flowers have a pleasant scent. Some flowers have no scent at all, and some others, such as the Corpse Flower, smell downright awful. Flowers usually emit scents to aid repopulation, or to attract pollinators. Some flowers use their aromas to entice various insects and birds to fertilise their flowers. Others are more specialized in their methods and release scents that only appeal to a particular insect. Fortunately, we are blessed with the ability to smell the scent of these flowers too. Some of the best smelling flowers include the Rose, Lily Of The Valley, Gardenia, Chocolate Cosmos, Sweet Pea and the Sweet Alyssum.
